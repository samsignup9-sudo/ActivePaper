Anaphase
Sister chromatids are pulled to opposite poles of the cell by spindle fibres.
Metaphase
Chromosomes align along the cell's equator, attached to spindle fibres at their centromeres.
Prophase
Chromosomes condense and become visible; the nuclear envelope begins to break down.
Interphase
The cell's resting state between divisions — DNA is replicated and the cell grows in preparation for mitosis.
