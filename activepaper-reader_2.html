Synaptic plasticity and long-term potentiation in hippocampal CA1 neurons following repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation

Chen et al. · Nature Neuroscience · 2024 · DOI: 10.1038/s41593-024-01621-x
One-liner: Repeated magnetic brain stimulation strengthens memory-related connections in a key brain region by activating a specific molecular signalling pathway.
Layers:
AI simplification
When brain cells fire together repeatedly, their connections get stronger — a process thought to underlie learning and memory. This study shows that non-invasive magnetic stimulation can trigger this strengthening in the hippocampus, by switching on a signalling cascade involving the BDNF protein.
